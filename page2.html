Start of it all

On November 1, 1946, in Toronto, Canada, the Toronto Huskies hosted the New York Knickerbockers at Maple Leaf Gardens, a game the NBA now regards as the first actual game played in the NBA history. People forget that the Knicks won that night, but the NBA then is considered boring to the current standards of the NBA. That game was from a different era of basketball when the scores would be low. Players did not instinctly double-pump or dunk. The truth is the players couldn't jump well and weren't "good" to todays players. Nor was there a 24-second clock; teams had unlimited time to shoot. The jump shot was a special and rare sight, because people thought that only bad things would happen if people left there feet to shoot.
